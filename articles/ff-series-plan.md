### FF-3: The 4% Rule Explained Simply
**Hook:** "In 1994, a financial researcher named William Bengen published a paper that quietly became the mathematical foundation of financial freedom for millions of people. Most people who quote it have never read it."
**Key content:**
- Bengen 1994 + Trinity Study 1998 explained simply
- What 95% success rate actually means
- Why 30 years ≠ Indian FIRE (50-year horizon → use 3.5%)
- Indian twist: 30–33x multiple (not 25x), inflation 6–7%
- PPF, NPS as Indian equivalents of tax-advantaged accounts
- Kabir's personal approach: conservative 3.5% for longer horizon
**Closing stat:** Median 55–64 year old in US has ~$95k — less than 2.5 years of expenses. Most people are nowhere near ready.

### FF-4: Why "Passive Income" Is Mostly a Lie
**Hook:** "I have met hundreds of people who told me they were building passive income. Very few of them were."
**Key content:**
- Define true passivity (index fund dividends = genuinely passive; everything else = varying degrees of work)
- Rental real estate reality: tenants, repairs, vacancies — NOT passive
- Online courses: constant updating, algorithm dependence
- Affiliate/content: platform risk, never set-and-forget
- What Kabir actually uses: diversified index fund portfolio, some direct equity
- The only two truly passive income sources: capital (index funds) + established IP
- Barbara Friedberg: "The only truly passive income is gift income"
**Indian context:** REITs in India (InvITs), SGBs, NPS annuity phase

### FF-5: What Your Childhood Taught You About Money
**Hook:** "My father never talked about money at home. Not once. And for years, I didn't either — until I realised that silence about money was itself a money belief."
**Key content:**
- Klontz Money Script Inventory — 4 scripts: Avoidance, Worship, Status, Vigilance
- Which correlates with wealth (Vigilance), which destroys it (Status)
- Indian money scripts: "money is not discussed," "rich people are corrupt," "save for security above all"
- Marshmallow test update: 2018 Watts replication — self-control is largely socioeconomic, not innate character
- Systems beat willpower (automation, defaults)
- Kabir's own script: grew up with money vigilance, which served him well; learned to enjoy spending only later
**Personal angle:** Journal entry on discovering his own money beliefs

### FF-6: Why I Put My Money in Boring Index Funds
**Hook:** "Over the 15-year period ending 2024, zero of 22 US equity fund categories had a majority of active managers outperform their benchmark. Zero."
**Key content:**
- SPIVA data: 90% of active large-cap funds underperform S&P 500 over 15 years
- SPIVA India 2024: 81.5% of large-cap funds underperformed Nifty
- Fee drag: 1% expense ratio = 16% cumulative drag over 15 years
- Index fund options in India: Nifty 50 index funds, Sensex index funds, Nifty Next 50, international index funds
- Kabir's portfolio philosophy: simple, diversified, low-cost
- JL Collins quote: "Spend less than you earn — invest the surplus — avoid debt"
- What to do with Kabir's portfolio: Nifty 50 + mid-cap index + small international allocation
**Key numbers:** ₹10,000/month in Nifty index fund at 12% nominal return for 25 years = ~₹1.9 crore

### FF-7: Lean FIRE, Fat FIRE, Coast FIRE — Which Path?
**Hook:** "Financial independence is not one destination. It is several — and choosing the right one for your life changes everything about how you get there."
**Key content:**
- Explain all 5 variants with Indian rupee numbers:
  - Lean FIRE: ₹8–12 lakh/year expenses → ₹2.4–3.6 crore corpus
  - Regular FIRE: ₹15–20 lakh/year → ₹4.5–6 crore
  - Fat FIRE: ₹30 lakh+/year → ₹9 crore+
  - Barista FIRE: part-time consulting + portfolio
  - Coast FIRE: stop saving, let existing investments compound
- Which one Kabir chose (effectively Barista/Fat — kept working by choice)
- Geographic arbitrage: Tier-2 Indian cities (Pune, Mysore, Coorg) vs metros
- The "retire to what" question
**Indian context:** Joint family advantage — shared costs reduce FIRE number significantly

### FF-8: Day Trading, MLMs, and Crypto — The Honest Statistics
**Hook:** "97% of people who tried day trading for more than 300 days lost money. Not most. Ninety-seven percent."
**Key content:**
- Day trading: Brazil study (Chague et al.) — 97% lost money after 300 days; only 1.1% earned above minimum wage
- FINRA: 72% of day traders end a year with losses
- MLM: 99% of participants lose money (FTC/Jon Taylor); average first-year loss ₹80,000–₹2.5 lakh
- Crypto speculation: identical psychology to day trading; lacks fundamental valuation anchors
- Why people fall for these (present bias, social proof, aspirational marketing)
- The comparison: patient index fund investor vs active speculator over 20 years
- Kabir's personal story: watched colleagues lose serious money to exactly these
**Closing:** The most boring investment strategy — regular SIPs in index funds — has beaten all of these over any 15-year period in India
