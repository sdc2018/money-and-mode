## Revenue Estimates (Realistic)

AdSense pays per 1,000 real visitors (RPM = revenue per mille).
For a lifestyle/finance blog from India, typical RPM is $4–12.

| Monthly Visitors | Est. Monthly AdSense |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| 5,000 | $20–60 |
| 10,000 | $40–120 |
| 25,000 | $100–300 |
| 50,000 | $200–600 |
| 100,000 | $400–1,200 |

**Plus affiliate income on top of AdSense:**
- 1 ChatGPT Plus referral/month = $20
- 1 Zerodha/Groww referral/month = ₹300–500
- 2 Amazon book sales/month = $2–5

At 25,000 visitors/month: total income could be $300–600/month ($3,600–7,200/year).
Not life-changing alone — but it validates the model and compounds.

## The Traffic Formula

```
Articles × Quality × Distribution = Traffic

Articles:    2/week × 52 weeks = 104 articles/year
Quality:     each article better than top 3 Google results
Distribution: Pinterest (3 pins) + Instagram + Quora answers + Twitter

Result:      exponential, not linear — each article adds to all previous ones
```

**Critical insight:** Blog traffic compounds. Article #50 doesn't just add to your traffic —
it also boosts articles #1–49 through internal links and domain authority.

The blogs that fail post 5 articles and wonder why nothing happened.
The blogs that succeed post 100+ articles and then wonder why traffic is suddenly everywhere.

## What Drives the Fastest Traffic Growth

In order of speed:
1. **Pinterest** — pins rank in search within days. Each pin = a potential traffic source for years.
2. **Quora answers** — answer a popular question = 50–200 visitors over months. Compounding.
3. **Google Search** — takes 3–6 months to start, then becomes the biggest source.
4. **Instagram** — brand building, not direct traffic driver. Important for trust.
5. **Email list** — returns existing visitors, doesn't bring new ones. High value, low volume.
