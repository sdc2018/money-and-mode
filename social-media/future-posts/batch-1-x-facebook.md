25 years in software, and the question I get asked most right now: "Will AI take my job?"

Here's the honest answer — not the headline-grabbing scary version, and not the "don't worry everything is fine" version.

The truth is in the middle.

AI won't take your entire job. It will automate specific tasks within your job. Whether that means you thrive or struggle depends entirely on how you respond.

Jobs where significant tasks are at risk:
• Data entry and repetitive processing
• Scripted customer service (the easy questions)
• Template-based writing and reporting
• Rule-based document review

Jobs where the core work is much safer:
• Roles requiring nuanced human judgment
• Anything involving complex relationships and trust
• Creative problem-solving across unknown situations
• Teaching, coaching, therapy — genuine human connection
• Skilled trades that require physical adaptability

The people most at risk aren't in the wrong industry. They're the ones assuming this doesn't apply to them and doing nothing about it.

What to do: identify which of your tasks are repetitive and predictable. Learn which AI tools exist in your field. Build the skills that AI genuinely can't replicate — judgment, empathy, creative synthesis.
